Recent News

  • May 8, 2019 - What a nearby kilonova would look like
    If two dense neutron stars collided relatively close to Earth, the resulting kilonova would shine day and night with the brightness of the moon squeezed into a small dot. Physicists imagined what we’d see if two neutron stars merged 1,000 light-years from Earth
  • May 2, 2019 - Two neutron stars collided near the solar system billions of years ago
    Astrophysicists Szabolcs Márka at Columbia University and Imre Bartos (GSAS'12) at the University of Florida have identified a violent collision of two neutron stars 4.6 billion years ago as the likely source of some of the most coveted matter on Earth.
