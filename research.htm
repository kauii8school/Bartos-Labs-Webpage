Research

Gravitational waves are cosmic messengers which are created by events of massive gavitational forces. Examples of these events are binary black holes, binary neutron stars, and spinning neutron stars. These waves were first predicted by Einstein's general theory of relativity but only recently have they been detected. LIGO and VIRGO are the interferometers which made this monumental discovery. The scale of these detectors is huge, with each of LIGO's arms spanning 4km. (Right: Picture of LIGO).
